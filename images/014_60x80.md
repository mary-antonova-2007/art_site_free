# Cave of the First Fire

The composition is filled with warm light, as if a flame were growing inside a stone cave. Ochre and terracotta forms rise along the sides, becoming ledges, stalactites, figures, and traces of ancient relief. At the center, a glowing space remains: entrance, hearth, or exposed core of the earth.

The work turns toward the primal memory of fire. The warmth is not domestic but cosmic; it illuminates stone from within and turns rough matter into a living altar. The painting speaks of the moment when darkness first becomes visible and fear of depth becomes a ritual of light.

## Visual Anchors

- Central glow.
- Ochre-orange rock forms.
- Cave-like depth.
- Rising rhythm of sharp fragments.

## Key Meanings

- Fire as the beginning of culture.
- Fear transformed into light.
- Earth depth as birthplace.
- Warmth awakening stone memory.
