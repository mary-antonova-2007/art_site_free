# Bird in a Winter Field

A slender bird stands among dry winter stems. Its silhouette is almost hieroglyphic: long legs, extended neck, small head, and a body touched by cold turquoise shimmer. Around it, the field becomes grass, frost, and fine cracks on glass at once.

This work is about the dignity of a small living being. The bird does not fight the space or try to dominate it; it simply holds its balance among fragile lines. Its stillness becomes a philosophy of survival: sometimes strength is not movement, but exact presence.

## Visual Anchors

- Solitary bird near the center.
- Transparent winter stems.
- Pale cold atmosphere.
- Copper and turquoise accents on the body.

## Key Meanings

- Fragility as endurance.
- Solitude without tragedy.
- Life inside winter silence.
- The calm precision of observation.
