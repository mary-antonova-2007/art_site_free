# Green Density of Life

The composition resembles a dense underwater garden or a wet forest seen from inside. Leaves, algae, corals, seed forms, and mineral stains fill almost the entire space. There is no emptiness here; the surface acts as living tissue where something constantly sprouts, rises, and dissolves.

The deeper meaning lies in abundance. Nature appears not as landscape but as a force that does not need a central hero. It is polyphonic, dense, dark, saturated, and endlessly productive. The work speaks of the world as a living medium connected by roots, water, and invisible breath.

## Visual Anchors

- Dense green surface.
- Botanical and underwater motifs.
- Many small textures.
- Absence of a single center.

## Key Meanings

- Life as a network of relations.
- Nature without human hierarchy.
- Underwater memory of the garden.
- Abundance as a philosophy of existence.
