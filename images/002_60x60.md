# Paleontology of a Dream

The image feels like a fragment of an ancient map lifted from water. A spiral shell rests near the center, surrounded by mineral shores, coral silhouettes, seaweed-like forms, and dark lace structures. The pale ground carries the softness of parchment, while the edges gather traces of depth and time.

This work speaks about memory older than human language. The shell becomes an inner archive: its turns preserve the motion of the sea, evolution, sleep, and return. Rather than depicting nature from outside, the image presents nature as a script written between water, stone, and body.

## Visual Anchors

- Spiral shell as the center of memory.
- Organic edges resembling a coastline map.
- Fine dark lace-like structures.
- Pale ground with an ancient-paper atmosphere.

## Key Meanings

- Time as a spiral.
- Nature as archive.
- The quiet presence of vanished life forms.
- The bond between water, stone, and memory.
