# Antique Soul of a Mineral

An antique head emerges from a dark blue depth, surrounded by crystals, shells, minerals, and feather-like fragments. The face is calm and distant, less like a portrait than a memory discovered inside stone. Around it, matter boils, splits, shines, and gathers in layers.

The work joins the human and the geological. Beauty here does not belong only to the body; it passes into stone, salt, shell, water, and metal. The image speaks of the soul as a mineral slowly formed under the pressure of time, preserving traces of both culture and nature.

## Visual Anchors

- Antique face at the center.
- Blue and white mineral forms.
- Dense surrounding crystals and organic fragments.
- Dark depth as a field of memory.

## Key Meanings

- Culture submerged in nature.
- The face as archaeological discovery.
- Beauty surviving ruin.
- The soul as crystallized experience.
